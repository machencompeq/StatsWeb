### Problem Statement
A marketing manager wants to determine if customer segment and preferred advertising channel are related. At the 0.05 level of significance, is there evidence of a relationship? Please utilize a complete S-P-D-C framework.
	Advertising Channel	
Customer Segment	Social Media	Email Campaign	Total
Millennial (18–35)	72	38	110
Gen X (36–55)	28	52	80
Total	100	90	190

### Model Solution
State: Hypotheses and Parameters   Goal: We want to determine if there is a significant relationship (dependence) between customer segment and advertising channel preference at the alpha = 0.05 significance level.   Hypotheses:   H0: Customer segment and advertising channel are independent (there is no relationship between them).   Ha: Customer segment and advertising channel are dependent (there is a significant relationship between them).   Significance Level: alpha = 0.05.

Plan: Procedure and Conditions   Procedure: Chi-square Test for Independence. To perform this test validly, we must verify:   Random: We assume the 190 customers represent a random sample from the target population.   Independence: The total sample size n = 190 is less than 10% of all customers.   Large Counts: All expected counts must be >= 5.   Expected Count (E) = (Row Total * Column Total) / Grand Total   Min Expected: (80 * 90) / 190 = 37.9   Since 37.9 >= 5, the condition is satisfied.

Do: Calculations   Data Summary:   Observed (O): Millennial/Social = 72, Millennial/Email = 38, GenX/Social = 28, GenX/Email = 52.   Expected (E): Millennial/Social = 57.9, Millennial/Email = 52.1, GenX/Social = 42.1, GenX/Email = 37.9.   Calculation:   Test Statistic (chi-square):   chi-square = Sum of ((O - E)^2 / E)   chi-square = (72 - 57.9)^2 / 57.9 + (38 - 52.1)^2 / 52.1 + (28 - 42.1)^2 / 42.1 + (52 - 37.9)^2 / 37.9   chi-square = 3.432 + 3.817 + 4.723 + 5.254 = 17.226   Degrees of Freedom (df): df = (rows - 1) * (cols - 1) = (2 - 1) * (2 - 1) = 1.   Two equivalent decision approaches may be used:   Approach 1 — Critical Value Method:   Critical Value: Using the Chi-square table for df = 1, alpha = 0.05: chi-square_critical = 3.841.   Decision Rule: Reject H0 if chi-square_stat > chi-square_critical.   Since 17.226 > 3.841 -> Reject H0.   Approach 2 — P-value Method:   P-value: For chi-square = 17.226 with df = 1, the p-value is approx. 0.000033 (< 0.0001).   Decision Rule: Reject H0 if p-value < alpha.   Since 0.000033 < 0.05 -> Reject H0.   Both approaches lead to the same conclusion.

Conclude: Interpretation   May use either of the two approaches below to state decision:   Approach 1 — Critical Value Method: Since chi-square = 17.226 > chi-square_critical = 3.841, the test statistic falls in the rejection region. We Reject H0 at alpha = 0.05.   Approach 2 — P-value Method: 
Since p-value approx. 0.000033 < alpha = 0.05, we Reject H0 at alpha = 0.05.   Contextual Conclusion: There is sufficient evidence at the 0.05 level of significance to conclude that there is a significant relationship between customer segment and preferred advertising channel. The marketing manager should tailor advertising strategies differently for Millennials (who prefer social media) and Gen X customers (who prefer email campaigns).
